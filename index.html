Changzhen Wang

I am a PhD candidate in Geography at Louisiana State University (LSU) under the direction of Dr. Fahui Wang. My doctoral study since the fall of 2018 has been awarded by the Economic Development Assistantship from LSU graduate school.

Before coming to LSU, I was a GIS project manager and developer at Wuhan Land Use and Urban Spatial Planning Research Center (WLSP), during which I served as a consultant at UN-Habitat in Nairobi, Kenya.

I obtained my Master’s degree of GIS and Cartography at Wuhan University and University of Michigan, Ann Arbor. I received my Bachelor’s degree of GIS at Southwest Jiaotong University in Chengdu, China.

Research Interests

My research interests are the development and applications of GIS, computational methods, spatial network science, geo-visualization in public health, urban studies, and transportation. My current research focuses on geospatial network analysis and visualization of health care service and the identification of spatial behaviors of patients and spatial structure of health care market.
